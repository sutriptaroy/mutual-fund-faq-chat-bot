# Sample Q&A

> All answers are facts-only and cite one official source.
> Opinion questions are politely refused.

---

**Q1.** What is the ELSS lock-in period?
**A.** ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) investments have a mandatory lock-in
period of 3 years from the date of allotment. Units cannot be redeemed during
this period.
*Source:* https://www.amfiindia.com/investor-corner/investor-center/elss-new.html
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*

---

**Q2.** What is an exit load?
**A.** An exit load is a fee charged by a mutual fund when an investor redeems
units before a specified period. The exact amount and applicable period vary
by scheme.
*Source:* https://www.sbimf.com/en-us/investor-services/exit-load
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*

---

**Q3.** What is the minimum SIP amount for SBI mutual funds?
**A.** Minimum SIP amounts vary by scheme; many SBI MF schemes accept SIPs
starting at ₹500 per month. Refer to the scheme page for the exact amount.
*Source:* https://www.sbimf.com/en-us/investor-services/sip
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*

---

**Q4.** What is a Riskometer?
**A.** The Riskometer is a SEBI-mandated visual indicator showing the level of
risk associated with a mutual fund scheme on a six-level scale from Low to
Very High. It is updated monthly.
*Source:* https://www.amfiindia.com/investor-corner/knowledge-center/Riskometer.html
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*

---

**Q5.** How do I download my SBI Mutual Fund account statement?
**A.** Account statements can be downloaded from the SBI MF investor services
portal using folio number / PAN authentication. They are also emailed
periodically to the registered email ID.
*Source:* https://www.sbimf.com/en-us/investor-services/account-statement
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*

---

**Q6.** What is the expense ratio?
**A.** The expense ratio is the annual fee, expressed as a percentage of the
fund's assets, charged by an AMC to manage the scheme. Both regular and direct
plans publish their expense ratios in the latest factsheet.
*Source:* https://www.sbimf.com/en-us/factsheet
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*

---

**Q7.** What is a benchmark?
**A.** A benchmark is a market index against which a mutual fund's performance
is measured. SEBI requires every scheme to declare an appropriate benchmark in
its Scheme Information Document.
*Source:* https://www.sebi.gov.in/sebi_data/faqfiles/jan-2017/1485345372625.html
*Last updated from sources: 2026-04-28*
